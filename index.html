  About Sl1me V0lt  

Our desired outcome is to successfully create living and growable resistors and capacitors to be eventually integrated into an interactive synthesizer. We want to create a system that promotes biological communication and opens creators and makers up to incorporating growable natural components into electronic systems in lieu of extracted metals and materials. We want to embrace an open-source model of information sharing so as to cultivate a web of information for future creators, we see this as important in making people comfortable and engaged with this kind of material. 

  Dr. Andrew Scarpelli  

 

Dr. Andrew Scarpelli is a Chicago science and art professor whose training is in microbiology and synthetic biology. He is a 2020 Global Community Biosummit fellow and SciArt Resident in “The Bridge” program. He is cofounder and president of the community bio-lab ChiTownBio. He is greatly interested in expanding the utility of biotechnology into fields such as art and social justice.
